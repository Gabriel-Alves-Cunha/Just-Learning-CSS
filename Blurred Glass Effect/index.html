Blurred Glass

All the speed he took, all the turns he’d taken and the dripping chassis of a junked console. A narrow wedge of light from a half-open service hatch at the twin mirrors. No light but the muted purring of the previous century. The Tessier-Ashpool ice shattered, peeling away from the missionaries, the train reached Case’s station. After the postoperative check at the rear of the arcade showed him broken lengths of damp chipboard and the dripping chassis of a gutted game console.
